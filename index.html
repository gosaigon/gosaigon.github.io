Explore Saigon's 22 Districts

Ho Chi Minh City is a sprawling metropolis, each district offering a unique flavor and experience. From the bustling downtown to serene green spaces, find your adventure here.

District 1

The vibrant heart of HCMC, home to iconic landmarks, luxury hotels, and bustling nightlife. Perfect for first-time visitors.

Worth visiting: Notre Dame Cathedral, Central Post Office, Ben Thanh Market, Independence Palace, Bui Vien Walking Street.

District 2 (Thủ Đức City)

Known for its expat community, modern cafes, international restaurants, and upscale residential areas. Now a part of Thủ Đức City.

Worth visiting: Thao Dien neighborhood, numerous international schools, riverside dining.

District 3

Combines historical charm with local life. Features beautiful French colonial villas and quieter streets than D1.

Worth visiting: Tan Dinh Church (Pink Church), War Remnants Museum, traditional coffee shops.

District 4

A former notorious area, now a vibrant, dense residential district known for its street food and local atmosphere.

Worth visiting: Vinh Khanh Street (seafood street), The Cafe Apartment (near border with D1).

District 5

Home to Saigon's historic Chinatown (Chợ Lớn), offering a rich cultural experience, ancient pagodas, and traditional markets.

Worth visiting: Binh Tay Market, Thien Hau Pagoda, herbal medicine shops.

District 6

Adjacent to District 5, it continues the authentic Chinese-Vietnamese cultural vibe with various temples and local markets.

Worth visiting: Cho Lon attractions, traditional craft shops.

District 7

A modern, affluent district known for its master-planned residential areas like Phu My Hung, popular with Koreans and expats.

Worth visiting: Crescent Mall, Starlight Bridge (Cầu Ánh Sao), international schools and hospitals.

District 8

A largely residential and industrial area, offering a glimpse into local working-class life along canals and bridges.

Worth visiting: Floating markets (seasonal), local temples.

District 9 (Thủ Đức City)

A rapidly developing area, part of the new Thủ Đức City, with tech parks, universities, and new residential complexes.

Worth visiting: Saigon Hi-Tech Park, Suoi Tien Theme Park (nearby).

District 10

A bustling commercial and residential hub, famous for its large markets, medical facilities, and vibrant street life.

Worth visiting: Ho Thi Ky Flower Market, Nguyen Tri Phuong Market, many hospitals.

District 11

Home to Dam Sen Cultural Park, this district is a mix of residential areas and local businesses.

Worth visiting: Dam Sen Cultural Park (theme park), Phu Tho Stadium.

District 12

A large, developing district in the northwest, primarily residential with expanding industrial zones.

Worth visiting: Thien An monastery, Binh Chieu market.

Bình Thạnh District

A diverse district with a mix of residential areas, universities, and commercial zones, often seen as a bridge between central and suburban areas.

Worth visiting: Landmark 81 (Vietnam's tallest building), Van Thanh Tourist Park, many local eateries.

Gò Vấp District

A populous residential district known for its local markets, affordable living, and proximity to Tan Son Nhat Airport.

Worth visiting: Local markets, traditional Vietnamese food stalls.

Phú Nhuận District

A small, lively district often traversed to and from the airport, with many local businesses, cafes, and residential streets.

Worth visiting: Phan Xich Long street (food street), many local cafes.

Tân Bình District

Home to Tan Son Nhat International Airport, it's a bustling area with hotels, restaurants, and residential zones.

Worth visiting: Tan Son Nhat Airport, Hoang Van Thu Park, many hotels and guesthouses.

Tân Phú District

A developing residential district with shopping malls and local amenities, offering a more suburban feel.

Worth visiting: AEON Mall Tan Phu Celadon, local markets.

Bình Tân District

The most populous district, located in the west, primarily residential and industrial with a strong local character.

Worth visiting: Go Vap Market, local street food.

Bình Chánh District

A large rural-urban district on the southwestern outskirts, rapidly urbanizing but still retaining agricultural areas.

Worth visiting: Local farms, traditional villages, Cu Chi Tunnels (further out).

Hóc Môn District

A large suburban district known for its historical significance during the Vietnam War and developing residential areas.

Worth visiting: Historical sites related to the Cu Chi Tunnels area.

Nhà Bè District

A southern suburban district, increasingly urbanized with new residential projects and access to rivers and canals.

Worth visiting: Phu Xuan Bridge, local seafood eateries along the river.

Cần Giờ District

A coastal district recognized as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, featuring mangrove forests, beaches, and wildlife.

Worth visiting: Can Gio Mangrove Forest (Monkey Island), Vam Sat Eco-tourism Park, 30/4 Beach.

Thủ Đức City

A newly established city formed by merging District 2, District 9, and Thủ Đức District, aiming to be an innovation hub and smart city.

Worth visiting: Thu Thiem Urban Area, Saigon Hi-Tech Park, National University HCMC.

Navigating Saigon: Motorbike Taxi Costs

Motorbike taxis, primarily via apps like GrabBike and Gojek, are the most common and convenient way to get around Ho Chi Minh City. Here's what you need to know about typical costs.

Short Rides (1-3 km)

Ideal for quick trips within a district or to an adjacent one.

Typical Cost: 15,000 VND - 30,000 VND (~$0.60 - $1.20 USD)

Medium Rides (3-8 km)

Covers trips across a few districts, e.g., District 1 to District 7.

Typical Cost: 30,000 VND - 60,000 VND (~$1.20 - $2.40 USD)

Long Rides (8+ km)

For longer journeys, such as from city center to Thủ Đức City or the airport.

Typical Cost: 60,000 VND - 120,000 VND+ (~$2.40 - $5.00+ USD)

When Prices Might Be Higher:

  • Peak Hours: During rush hour (e.g., 7-9 AM, 5-7 PM), prices can surge due to high demand.
  • Heavy Rain: Rides become more expensive and harder to book during heavy downpours.
  • Special Events/Holidays: Fares may increase during major festivals or public holidays.
  • Dynamic Pricing: Grab and Gojek use dynamic pricing, so prices can fluctuate based on real-time demand and supply. Always check the app's estimated fare before confirming.

Always use the in-app fare displayed on GrabBike or Gojek. This ensures you pay the correct price and avoids overpaying.
